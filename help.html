GET HELP

You are not alone. Help is available, and recovery is possible. If you or someone you know is struggling with drug abuse, reach out for support immediately.

Why Getting Help Matters

Drug addiction is a serious condition that affects both physical and mental health. Seeking help early can prevent long-term damage, improve quality of life, and increase the chances of full recovery. There is no shame in asking for help — it is a brave and important step.

Common Signs of Drug Addiction

Behavior Changes

Sudden mood swings, secrecy, isolation, and loss of interest in daily activities.

Physical Symptoms

Weight loss, tiredness, red eyes, poor hygiene, and unusual body reactions.

Life Problems

Declining school/work performance, financial issues, and relationship conflicts.

Where to Get Help

Family & Friends

Talk to someone you trust. Support from loved ones is very important in recovery.

Medical Professionals

Doctors and counsellors can provide treatment, therapy, and medical support.

Rehabilitation Centres

Specialised centres provide structured recovery programs and long-term support.

Helplines (Malaysia)

Talian Kasih

15999
Available for counselling and support services.

National Anti-Drug Agency (AADK)

Contact your nearest AADK office for treatment, rehabilitation, and guidance.

Emergency Help

If someone is in immediate danger, call emergency services or go to the nearest hospital.

You Are Not Alone

Recovery takes time, but it is possible. Every step you take toward getting help is a step toward a better life. Do not hesitate to reach out — support is always available.
